Related Mesothelioma Compensation Information

All victims of asbestos exposure diagnosed with mesothelioma are entitled to financial compensation. This compensation can come from settlements, verdicts, or awards from asbestos trust funds. These funds have been established to provide financial support to victims and their families impacted by mesothelioma.

Who is eligible for mesothelioma compensation?

Anyone diagnosed with mesothelioma who can prove they were exposed to asbestos through no fault is eligible for mesothelioma compensation. This includes workers who were exposed to asbestos on the job, family members of workers who brought home asbestos fibers on their clothing, and individuals who lived near asbestos mines or factories.

What factors are considered when determining mesothelioma compensation?

The amount of compensation a victim of mesothelioma is eligible for depends on many factors. These factors include the severity of the victim's illness, age, length of time they were exposed to asbestos, the extent of their suffering, and financial losses. In addition, the compensation amount will also take into account the victim's medical expenses, lost wages, and other damages.

What is the average amount of mesothelioma compensation?

The average amount of mesothelioma compensation can vary, depending on the specific circumstances of each case. Some victims may receive compensation as a settlement, ranging from a few thousand dollars to millions of dollars. Others may accept a verdict, which is a court-ordered award. The average mesothelioma compensation award is typically in the range of $1 million to $2 million.

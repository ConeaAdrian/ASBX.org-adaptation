Guide to the Owens Corning Fibreboard Asbestos Personal Injury Trust

The Owens Corning Fibreboard Asbestos Personal Injury Trust, established in 2006, serves as a key legal resolution platform for all asbestos-related claims against Owens Corning and its subsidiary, Fibreboard. This comprehensive guide provides detailed insights into the Trust's purpose, operations, and how it resolves claims following the Trust Distribution Procedures (TDP).

Introduction

The Owens Corning Fibreboard Asbestos Personal Injury Trust is a notable case of a legal arbitration system. This Trust was put in place on October 31, 2006, and stems from the bankruptcy of two leading companies, Owens Corning and its wholly-owned subsidiary, Fibreboard.

Origins of the Trust

Both companies, Owens Corning and Fibreboard, faced bankruptcy, which led to the foundation of the Owens Corning Fibreboard Asbestos Personal Injury Trust. This Trust was to address all asbestos-related lawsuits that both businesses were legally responsible for.

Purpose of the Trust

The Trust was primarily established to resolve all existing asbestos-related legal disputes associated with Owens Corning, Fibreboard, and any other relevant protected parties.

Start of Claim Acceptance

The Trust initiated the acceptance of claims related to Owens Corning Fibreboard Asbestos Personal Injury on August 27, 2007, starting precisely at 10:00 AM EST.

Function and Process of the Trust

The Trust operates with the main function of recognizing and settling all valid asbestos personal injury claims filed against the relevant parties. The procedure it follows aligns with a specific protocol known as the Trust Distribution Procedures (TDP).

Trust Distribution Procedures (TDP)

The Trust Distribution Procedures (TDP) is a protocol approved by the bankruptcy court for handling and distributing asbestos personal injury claims.

Payment Policies

The Trust follows a clear and precise payment policy for the settlement of claims.

Payment Percentage for Owens Corning

For Owens Corning (O.C.), the current payment percentage stands at 5.9% of the scheduled value for an Expedited Review. The same rate, 5.9%, applies to the total settlement value for Individualized Review.

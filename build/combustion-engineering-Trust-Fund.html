Guide to the Combustion Engineering 524(g) Asbestos PI Trust

The Combustion Engineering 524(g) Asbestos PI Trust is a fund established to handle asbestos injury claims. The Trust evaluates, liquidates, and pays valid claims linked to Combustion Engineering's past asbestos use. The fund continues to operate today, accepting new claims for asbestos-related injuries.

Introduction to Combustion Engineering Asbestos PI Trust

The Combustion Engineering 524(g) Asbestos PI Trust, known as "the Trust," was instituted as part of Combustion Engineering Inc.'s (CE) Reorganization Plan, as modified through October 7, 2005. The Trust's primary role is to evaluate, liquidate, and disburse payments for valid asbestos personal injury claims as per the Combustion Engineering 524(g) Asbestos PI Trust Distribution Procedures (TDP).

In alignment with the TDP, the Trust has developed a comprehensive set of policies and procedures for receiving, reviewing, and settling claims related to asbestos personal injuries. A mandatory claim form constitutes the proof of claim filed with the Trust.

Creation of Combustion Engineering's Asbestos Trust Fund

In 2003, the ABB Group claimed Chapter 11 bankruptcy for Combustion Engineering to deal with the company's asbestos-related financial obligations. However, in October 2005, an agreement was reached, establishing the Combustion Engineering 524(g) Asbestos Personal Injury Trust. Asbestos Usage by Combustion Engineering

Combustion Engineering engaged in the manufacturing, utilizing, and distribution of a range of asbestos-containing products from the 1930s to the mid-1970s. Some of the company's earliest asbestos products included:

  • Welded boilers with asbestos insulation

  • Asbestos-containing insulation materials

  • Asbestos-infused cement materials

Boilers, found in over 3,000 locations across many US states, were employed by corporations such as Proctor & Gamble, Owens-Illinois, Inc., and Monsanto Chemical Company. Moreover, asbestos products were widely used in the shipbuilding industry, appearing at shipyards, including Alabama Drydock & Shipping Company and several Bethlehem Steel shipyards. Several products produced by Combusting Engineering are well-documented to contain asbestos:

  • Block Stick

  • Combustion Engineering Boilers

  • Buck Stay Cement A- 1360

  • Calcrete Insulating Cement

  • Castablock Insulating Cement

  • Expansion Joint Hat

Occupational Asbestos Exposure and Risks

Due to Combustion Engineering's hazardous products, numerous professions were prone to occupational asbestos exposure. Workers directly engaged with asbestos maintenance and production likely faced exposure to harmful minerals, particularly during maintenance or material removal tasks. Boilermakers faced significant risks while dismantling asbestos-containing components like insulation and cement. Professions exposed to asbestos include:

  • Boilermaker/Boiler-tender

  • Industrial/Commercial Construction

  • Residential Construction

  • Machinist/Machinist Mate

  • Machinist/Machinist Mate

  • Maintenance Mechanic

  • Maritime/Deckhand

  • Railroad

  • Steelworker

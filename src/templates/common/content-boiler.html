Boiler-Related Occupations and Asbestos Exposure

The world of boiler-related occupations, such as boilermakers and boiler tenders, is steeped in a rich history of industrial progress. These professionals, often cloaked in the mantle of steam and steel, are the builders of our modern world, ensuring the efficient operation of power plants, ships, and factories. However, there's a hidden facet to these jobs that the mist of steam has obscured: the danger of asbestos exposure.

Asbestos, a heat-resistant material, was extensively used in boiler installations and maintenance for years. While it proved effective in heat insulation, its tiny fibers, when inhaled, posed severe health risks, including mesothelioma, lung cancer, and other asbestos-related diseases. This dark underbelly of boiler-related jobs is a stark reminder of how progress often comes at a cost.

Asbestos Exposure and Mesothelioma Risk abroad USS Currituck (AV-7)

The USS Currituck (AV-7) was a seaplane tender built at the Philadelphia Navy Yard and named after the Currituck Sound in North Carolina. The ship's construction began on December 14, 1942, and it was launched on September 11, 1943. The USS Currituck was commissioned on June 26, 1944, and served the United States Navy until it was decommissioned on October 31, 1967. The vessel was eventually stricken from the Naval Vessel Register on April 1, 1971, and sold for scrapping later that year. During its time in service, the USS Currituck (AV-7) was constructed using various asbestos-containing materials, which were commonly used in shipbuilding at the time. These materials were known for their heat resistance, insulation, and fireproofing properties, making them ideal for use in naval vessels. However, the presence of asbestos onboard the USS Currituck (AV-7) put the crew at risk of developing mesothelioma from asbestos exposure.
